Raw thoughts from Alex Dong

How unique is a flower?

Life is fuzzy and poetic.

Numbers are clean and precise.

We should develop the muscle to appreciate both. After all, we are born with a right brain for intuition and a left brain for logic thinking. Why should we only use one? Why should we pit one against the other. It's to wonderful to appreciate that every flower is unique. It's equally great fun to know just exactly how unique is a flower?

I'm a big fan of modeling and I believe that the process of building a model helps us to gain much clarity into a topic. It gives us a sense of agency. Instead of feeling powerless and at the mercy of a giant random number generator in the sky, a good model helps us to understand our world better so we know where our time and effort will give the best return.

Here are a few excellent examples of a great model. Each takes a thorny question tangled up with social and morale implications and put a clearly defined number on them. I find them fascinating and I hope you enjoy them too.

First is how much is Facebook worth to a user? Answer: $1000 per year. Quoting from the paper:

Facebook, the online social network, has more than 2 billion global users. Because those users do not pay for the service, its benefits are hard to measure. We report the results of a series of three non-hypothetical auction experiments where winners are paid to deactivate their Facebook accounts for up to one year. Though the populations sampled and the auction design differ across the experiments, we consistently find the average Facebook user would require more than $1000 to deactivate their account for one year. While the measurable impact Facebook and other free online services have on the economy may be small, our results show that the benefits these services provide for their users are large.
